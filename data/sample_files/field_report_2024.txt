FIELD REPORT — COLOMBIA
Date: 2024-03-22
Author: Maria Fernandez, Field Program Officer
Program: Livelihoods
Region: Chocó Department

SITUATION OVERVIEW
The Chocó department continues to experience significant displacement due to armed conflict,
affecting approximately 12,000 people across 8 municipalities. Our Livelihoods program is
targeting 1,200 households in the most affected areas with vocational training and start-up
capital grants to help families rebuild economic stability.

PROGRAM PROGRESS
During Q1 2024, we successfully enrolled 340 households in the vocational training component.
Training tracks include artisanal fishing techniques, sustainable agriculture, and micro-enterprise
management. Dropout rates remain low at 6%, attributed to the childcare support provided at
training venues. Cash disbursements of USD 250 per household have been completed for 280 of
the enrolled families, with the remaining 60 disbursements scheduled for early April.

CHALLENGES
Road access to two communities (Riosucio and Juradó) was disrupted following heavy rainfall in
February, delaying distribution activities by approximately three weeks. Logistics costs have
increased by 18% as a result. We are in discussion with local partners to explore river-based
transport alternatives.

NEXT STEPS
- Complete remaining cash disbursements by April 10
- Begin Phase 2 enrollment targeting 200 additional households
- Conduct post-distribution monitoring visits in March cohort communities
- Submit quarterly financial report to donor by April 30
